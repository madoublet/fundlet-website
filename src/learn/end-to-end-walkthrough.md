## Overview

This walkthrough covers everything in one go — creating a campaign, setting up your storefront, onboarding participants, and reviewing results. It's a great starting point if you're new to Fundlet, or a useful refresher before launching a new campaign.

---

## Step 1: Create the Campaign

1. Open Fundlet from your Shopify admin.
2. Click **New Campaign**.
3. Enter the campaign name, description, and upload a cover photo.
4. Set your fundraising goal and payout structure (flat percentage or per-product amounts).
5. Set a start and end date if you want the campaign time-limited.
6. Click **Save** (the campaign stays in draft until you activate it).

---

## Step 2: Set Up Your Storefront

1. In the campaign, go to the **Storefront** tab.
2. Link the campaign to a Shopify page or collection that will serve as the landing page.
3. Open **Online Store → Themes → Customize** and add the Fundlet app blocks to the page template — the campaign header, goal progress bar, and product grid.
4. Save the theme.
5. Preview the page to make sure it looks right before going live.

---

## Step 3: Add Participants

1. Back in the campaign, go to the **Participants** tab.
2. Click **Add Participant** and enter their name, email, and optionally a photo.
3. Fundlet generates a unique shareable link for each participant automatically.
4. Share each participant's link with them — when supporters visit via that link, the participant is credited for the sale.

For larger groups, use [CSV upload](/learn/upload-participant-csv) to add many participants at once.

---

## Step 4: Activate the Campaign

1. Return to the campaign overview.
2. Click **Activate**.
3. The campaign is now live — the storefront is visible and participant links are active.

---

## Step 5: Monitor Sales

During the campaign, check the dashboard to see:

- Total revenue raised so far
- Progress toward the goal
- Sales broken down by participant
- Individual order details

---

## Step 6: Close Out and Export

When the campaign ends:

1. Set the campaign status to **Closed** (or let it expire automatically if you set an end date).
2. Go to the **Reports** tab and export the CSV.
3. The report includes every order, the participant it was attributed to, and any custom field responses.
4. Use the report to calculate and distribute payouts to the organization.
